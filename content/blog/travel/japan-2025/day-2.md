Today's agenda was simple, shopping. I need to buy some accessories for the cold weather here and in preparation for the snow, and I also wanted to do some perusing for some gifts and souvenirs.

I had a bit of sleep in this morning, thanks to the black out blinds in the hotel. The first things that I had on my list to buy was a warm top layer because my jumpers simply will not be warm enough, and some film for my camera. I needed both of these before I could go anywhere.

Uniqlo Ginza was stop number one where I would pick up a nice warm jacket that I could wear over my jumpers. This Uniqlo never ceases to amaze me with it's 14 floors, though travelling up all of the escalators sure takes some time. I walked around the rest of the store to see if there was anything else I could do with, but I only ended up picking up a jacket.

I then headed to Nakano for Fujiya Film where I picked up some film. I wasn't sure what stock I wanted and was contemplating a few but thankfully they had a sample pack that had 3 different rolls, 2 of these were 200's which I read would be okay for the snow.

My digital camera that I also brought had been strapless for years, so I headed to the infamous Yodobashi-Akiba in Akihabara so I could grab a strap and make sure my camera was secure. I wandered around this massive store for a while to see if there was anything exciting new since I had been last. After I headed back to the hotel for a bit of a nap.

![](/blog/travel/japan-2025/images/IMG_6058.jpeg)

I wanted to do some more exploring around Tokyo and see how the running situation would be, last time I got stopped at the Imperial Palace by the police so I didn't want this to happen again. I wandered around Ginza and the surrounding area. I stopped in at Nike to grab a running jacket as I had forgotten to pack one in my bag, I forgot how cold it was going to be!

![](/blog/travel/japan-2025/images/IMG_6067.jpeg)

I ended the night with a stop in the conbini to grab some dinner and a drink and then headed back to the hotel for an early night before my trip to Lake Kawaguchiko tomorrow.

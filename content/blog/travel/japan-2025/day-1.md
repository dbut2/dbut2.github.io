Today was a day I had been dreading, a 5am wakeup to catch buses, trams, trains, and planes to make it to Tokyo. This was made less dreadful by a message I received from Qantas yesterday morning letting me know my flight had been upgrade to Business Class. I was now looking forward to what was going to be the longest and worst part of the day.

My previous flights to and from Japan had all been overnight flights which meant that I could spend at least some of the 10 hour flight asleep and reset a bet. Sitting in my own little walled off seat with no one next to me would make this much easier to handle.

I started the morning with packing the last of my bags with all my charged electronics and then bussed and trammed to the airport. Thanks to my upgrade I now had access to the business lounge before departing so I snuck in a couple beers in comfort despite it still being early morning.

![](/blog/travel/japan-2025/images/IMG_5653.jpeg)

Fortunately the flight was not delayed despite the previous week of this flight being so, this was nice. I was able to promptly head from the lounge to the gate and line straight up for priority boarding ahead of everyone else on the plane and was able to get to my sit and get settled while the rest of the plane boarded.

I was met by one the crew who gave me a welcome drink to sip on before the plane taxied out, and took my food orders for the rest of the flight (no need to hope there was something left on the cart by the time it made it down). The rest of the flight was a breeze, a few beers here and there upon request, and some delicious meals had. I had a beef fillet for lunch and desert was an ice cream sandwich that was the best I've ever had. I spent most of the time just playing my switch.

![](/blog/travel/japan-2025/images/IMG_6038.jpeg)

![](/blog/travel/japan-2025/images/IMG_6042.jpeg)

We finally arrived into Tokyo Narita at about 6pm, being winter it was already dark so I couldn't see much of the city as we flew in. I got through immigration mostly fine and was able to collect my bags as soon as I got to the carriage thanks to the business priority tag on my bag.

![](/blog/travel/japan-2025/images/IMG_6050.jpeg)

After getting through the airport I hopped on a train to Tokyo. Thanks to flying in to Narita this train took about an hour and a half before I got to my hotel but I was able to check-in quickly, head the conbini for a quick sweet treat and then head to bed as I was very tired.

Today was a day of peace, or zen, and was spent travelling out to Mt Fuji where I will stay the night. I started with an early morning getting my bags packed for the night and out of the hotel by 10am so they could do some cleaning in the room.

I had a bus booked at 12:45 from Shinjuku that would take me to Mt Fuji and I had a couple hours to kill until then. I hopped on the train headed for Shinjuku and briefly hopped off at Roppongi for a bite to eat for breakfast. This didn't quite kill enough time so I planned to head to the bus stop, drop my bags in a locked and then do some wandering until it was time to catch the bus.

On the train I decided to hop off a station early at Yoyogi and instead just walk the rest of the way in the hopes I could kill some time, I knew that there were a few parks nearby so I could walk through those for a bit. I ended up stumbling into the entrance of Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden and thought this would be the perfect place.

I wandered around here for about an hour, walking through all of the gardens they had, and through a greenhouse they had set up where there was some beautiful flowers. It was very warm and humid in there! A grabbed a drink to eat from the Starbucks as they seem to have these in all of their parks, and then headed for Shinjuku station so find the bus stop.

![](/blog/travel/japan-2025/images/IMG_6214.jpeg)

When I got to the station I was able to find _a_ bus stop that was almost named what my ticket had said on it but was missing the "South" part, because of this I wasn't quite sure if this was where I was meant to be, and there was also 4 bus stations marked A-D, but this wasn't listed on my ticket so I was a little stressed. I found a departures board inside where I eventually found the bus I was catching along with what station it was leaving from and I was put at ease.

The bus trip was very scenic traveling through the mountains and country side along the way to Kawaguchiko. Seeing Mt Fuji creep in from afar was mesmerising. My stop was the last on the route, and everyone else had gotten off at Kawaguchiko Station but I was travelling a little further to one of the nearby towns so it was just me and the bus driver for another 20 minutes.

I eagerly hopped off the bus and beelined to the shore where I could see Mt Fuji in all of it's beauty, not covered by any other mountains, towns, or anything else in the way. It was at this point I realised I left my Switch on the bus. I quickly ran back to see if the bus was still there but alas he was gone. I will need to call the bus company tomorrow.

![](/blog/travel/japan-2025/images/IMG_6558.jpeg)

After taking some pictures at the lake I headed for my stay for the night. I didn't realise it was so far away from the town and would another 20 minute walk with my heavy bag. I ended up walking through what felt like the countryside with some rice fields and farms and sparse homes.

The guesthouse I was staying at was very quiet, I think it might just be me here tonight. I checked in with the lady at reception and I ordered some dinner and breakfast in the morning. I was originally planning on going to a FamilyMart or finding something similar but when I was in town before it looks like I would've had to go back to Kawaguchiko. I also booked some time in for the private bathhouse they have here, which is very needed now that I'm here in negative temperature for the first time.

I settled in for the night and unpacked my bag. I took some nice photos of Mt Fuji thanks to having a direct view from my room. I headed back downstairs to have some dinner, and I was the only one there. The lady from reception brought out my dinner and it looked like it was home made. It was very delicious, oishi.

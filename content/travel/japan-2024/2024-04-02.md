Good morning! Today I write this entry alone because yesterday was officially the last day of devhouse. We started off the day by cleaning up the house and packing our bags. Damian and Bailey had already left in the early hours before we woke up.

Once devhouse was all tidy and our bags were all packed, we said our fairwells to the house and locked the keys away. Our first stop for the day was to go straight to the train station and book tickets for the Shinkansen back to Tokyo as James, Elise and I all had the be back there.

The tickets were a little confusing to buy and I almost didn't end up with the right ticket. We eventually got them sorted, grabbed a bite to eat for the journey and hopped on the train. It was a peaceful ride, and James and I had spent some time working on some final changes to the devhouse website.

James built a merch store, which you should visit [shop.devhou.se](https://shop.devhou.se).

![image](https://github.com/devhou-se/www-jp/assets/61171623/70356b86-83e2-42a6-9f97-ce028c743950)

While on the train we were fortunate enough to get a nice and clear view of Mount Fuji. It was spectacular!

When we arrived in Tokyo, we had to find some scales for James to use so he would know how many giga strong zeros he could take back. We ended up using a set of display scales and he was within his weight limits.

![image](https://github.com/devhou-se/www-jp/assets/61171623/db7eec2c-8b2b-410c-b202-01f2b30e426e)

After this we said our last farewells, James had to head for the airport, and Elise and I had to separately check into our hotels.

Once I checked in, I went for a walk and explored the area. It's nice being back in Tokyo with all of the Sakura blooming, it's very pretty.

![image](https://github.com/devhou-se/www-jp/assets/61171623/bee724c7-4b4a-45fc-a2d0-c6445ccb6af6)

I look forward to the next few days staying here, I think I want to visit some more parks to view the blooming Sakura, and maybe I will visit some museums and do some more sight seeing.

Devhouse Tokyo edition has been an amazing trip, and I'll remember this trip for a very long time.

Until next time! See you in Bali!
